My On-Call Shifts
=================

An at-a-glance view of your on-call shifts

My On-Call Shifts allows users to view their current and upcoming on-call responsibilities in a single, easy-to-use view, where they can also create overrides for any of their shifts. My On-Call Shifts is also available as a widget on the right side of the Incidents page, which displays a userâ€™s current shift and when they are on call next.

> ðŸ“˜ Schedules and Escalation Policies
> -----------------------------------
> 
> My On-Call Shifts displays schedule shifts if the schedule is part of an active [escalation policy](/main/docs/escalation-policies). If you have shifts on a schedule that *do not* appear in My On-Call Shifts, please check that the schedule on an escalation policy, and that a service uses the escalation policy.

My On-Call Shifts in the Web App
================================

To access the My On-Call Shifts page:

1. Navigate to **People**  **My On-Call Shifts**, or **User Icon**  **My Profile**  **On-Call Shifts** tab.

The following information is displayed here:

* **Currently On-Call**: In the upper left you will see shifts that you are currently on call for.
* **My On-Call Shifts**: Below on the left, you will see a list of all your on-call shifts.
* **Calendar**: Center screen you will see a calendar of your upcoming on-call shifts, up to 90 days in the future.
  + Use the dropdown in the top right to adjust the time range by **month**, **week**, **day** or **list** view.

> ðŸ“˜ 90 Day Calendar Limit
> -----------------------
> 
> The calendar will only display shifts up to 90 days in the future. You may view calendar dates beyond 90 days, but shifts will not be visible.

Shift Types
-----------

Shifts types are denoted by the following:

* Recurring on-call shifts have a **solid color**.

![Recurring on-call shift with a solid color](https://files.readme.io/8c7b27f-image2.png "image2.png")

* Shifts where you are always on-call have a **diagonal line pattern**.

![Always on-call shift with a diagonal line pattern](https://files.readme.io/3fc0e20-image3.png "image3.png")

Adjust the Time Zone
--------------------

You can adjust the time zone using the dropdown on the left side of the screen.

![Adjust the time zone using the dropdown](https://files.readme.io/d96c485-image7.png "image7.png")

Toggle Escalation Policy Layers
-------------------------------

To add or remove on-call shifts from the view, click the **eye icon** to the right of a shift in your **My On-Call Shifts** list. Your selection is saved when you navigate away from the page.

![Toggle escalation policy layers](https://files.readme.io/91c6635-my-on-call-shifts-edit-shift-view.png "my-on-call-shifts-edit-shift-view.png")

Create Overrides on My On-Call Shifts
=====================================

There are two ways to create overrides on the My On-Call Shifts page:

* [Use the Multi-Overrides tool](/main/docs/my-on-call-shifts#multi-overrides-tool)
* [Use the Create Override button](/main/docs/my-on-call-shifts#create-override-button)

Multi-Overrides Tool
--------------------

> ðŸ“˜ Delete Overrides Made Using the Multi-Overrides Tool
> ------------------------------------------------------
> 
> * If you wish to delete any overrides that were made with the Multi-Overrides tool, you will need to individually delete them. This is typically done on the associated scheduleâ€™s detail page. This Knowledge Base article covers [deleting overrides](/main/docs/editing-schedules#delete-an-override).
> * Multi-Overrides can only be made up to 3 months out from the current date.

1. Navigate to **People**  **My On-Call Shifts**.
2. In the calendar, hover over a date, then click and drag to select the range of dates you would like to override.

![Click and drag to select a date range](https://files.readme.io/7cc1ad0-image5.png "image5.png")

3. In the modal, select a user from the **Who should take this shift?** dropdown.
4. Review the shifts that you are overriding â€” you may *optionally* edit the start and end times, as well as uncheck shifts from the list at the bottom of the modal.

![Create Override](https://files.readme.io/ebcb218-image6.png "image6.png")

5. Click **Create Override**.

Create Override Button
----------------------

1. Navigate to **People**  **My On-Call Shifts**  **Create Override**.

![Create Override button](https://files.readme.io/55353db-image1.png "image1.png")

2. In the modal, select a user to override In the **Who should take this shift?** dropdown, select a user to override the shift.
3. Update the start and end times to cover the appropriate time range.

* *Optional*: If you do not wish to override all of a userâ€™s shifts, you can uncheck shifts from the list at the bottom of the modal.

4. Click **Create Override**.
