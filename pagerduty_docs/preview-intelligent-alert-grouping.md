Preview Intelligent Alert Grouping
==================================

Preview how the Intelligent Alert Grouping algorithm would automatically group existing alerts

The Preview Intelligent Alert Grouping feature gives service owners an overview of how [Intelligent Alert Grouping](/main/docs/intelligent-alert-grouping) would have grouped historic alerts into incidents. This information can help inform how Intelligent Alert Grouping will group future alerts, since automatically grouped alerts mean fewer overall incidents and interruptions for responders.

View the Preview Intelligent Alert Grouping Feature
===================================================

Services with a high volume of incidents, and some variance in their incident titles, tend to have the most interesting and meaningful previews.

You can find the Preview Intelligent Alert Grouping feature by selecting an appropriate service in the web app:

1. Navigate to **Services**  **Service Directory** and select the **name** of your desired service.
2. Select the **Settings** tab, scroll to the **Reduce Noise** section, and click **Edit**/**New Grouping**.
3. With **Intelligent** selected, click **Preview**.

![An image showing a "Preview" link for Intelligent Alert Grouping](https://files.readme.io/3f3ee84-preview_iag.png "Select Intelligent Alert Grouping")



Select Preview link

Preview Details
---------------

The preview modal provides a rich view of how alerts would have been grouped into incidents over a given period of time. Previews are generated once a day and are based on alerts from the past 45 days.

The preview indicates the number of incidents that would have triggered without alert grouping, and how many fewer incidents your team would have been notified for if Intelligent Alert Grouping had been enabled. The preview assumes a five-minute time window unless a time window with a different duration has been set for that service.

You will see metrics at the top of the preview, which are all calculated based on:

* **Alerts**: The total number of [Alerts](/main/docs/alerts) this service has seen within the past 45 days.
* **Incidents**: The total number of incidents within the past 45 days this service would have seen without turning on any alert grouping.
* **Number of Unique Incidents**: The number of incidents that would have been triggered within the past 45 days, if the service had had Intelligent Alert Grouping turned on.
* **Number of Incidents Saved**: The number of incidents that would have been saved from a trigger within the past 45 days, if the service had had Intelligent Alert Grouping turned on.

![A screenshot of the PagerDuty UI detailing the Preview Intelligent Alert Grouping feature, including graphs and statistics](https://files.readme.io/7aad8a6-preview-iag-modal.png "Preview Intelligent Alert Grouping")



Preview Intelligent Alert Grouping



You can expand each alert group to see how many, and which, alerts would have been grouped together into an incident. The date and time listed reflect when the first incoming alert was triggered. Please note that the incident's title is derived from the first incoming alert. Read more about this in the [Alerts](/main/docs/alerts#alerts-and-incident-titles) article.

Some services may have a large number of alert groups in this list. We recommend looking at some of the larger groups to get a sense of the grouping algorithm’s behavior. To learn more about the grouping algorithm, please see [Intelligent Alert Grouping](/main/docs/intelligent-alert-grouping#algorithm-behavior).

FAQ
===

Expand/Collapse All

I just triggered an incident/alert, why is it not appearing in the preview?
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Previews are not generated in real time. They are generated once a day. If you have new alert data that you’d like the preview to take into account, please check back in 24 hours, after the next preview has been generated.

When will the preview on my service be updated?
-----------------------------------------------

New previews are calculated once a day, provided there have been sufficient alerts in the last few months. If a preview is unavailable on your service, it could be because the Intelligent Alert Grouping algorithm doesn’t have enough data to generate a new preview.

Existing previews are only deleted when a new preview is available to replace it.

Some alerts appear under incidents that I wouldn’t want to be grouped together. How can I adjust these groups?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

While the preview is static and cannot be changed directly, you can still provide feedback on the quality of the groups you see and this feedback will help the algorithm learn and create better groups.

To provide feedback on the groups, go to the original incident and assign the alert to another incident, or merge incidents together. Please read [Editing Incidents](/main/docs/edit-incidents#merge-incidents) for more information about moving alerts from one incident to another.

After moving alerts and/or merging incidents, your changes will not be immediately reflected in the current preview, however you may notice a difference in the next preview.

Why do I see fewer alert groups than expected?
----------------------------------------------

The preview's table displays alert groups that have two or more alerts. Furthermore, only the top 10 alert groups are displayed, sorted by alert count.
