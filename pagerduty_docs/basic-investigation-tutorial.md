Basic Investigation Tutorial
============================

Learn how to effectively prepare for your incident review with Jeli Opportunities | Jeli Part of PagerDuty

> 📘 Availability
> --------------
> 
> Jeli is available to customers with a standalone Jeli plan, or the Enterprise plan for Incident Management.
> 
> Please contact our [Sales Team](https://www.pagerduty.com/contact-us/#contact-sales) to upgrade to an account plan with Jeli.

Prep for Your Incident Review Meeting
=====================================

⏰ **Time required**: 30 mins to 1 hour

In this tutorial, you will learn how to prepare for your incident review meeting. You'll review your Slack transcript to create a **Narrative** timeline of your incident, prepare **Questions** you want to ask during the meeting, and capture a preliminary set of **Action Items**. These three topics will make up the agenda of your review meeting.

Step 1: Create the Jeli Opportunity and Gather Evidence
-------------------------------------------------------

Create your Jeli opportunity by navigating to the Slack channel you'd like to import and use the `/jeli import` command. If you're using Jeli's incident response bot, or if you've connected Jeli to internal response tooling via the Jeli API, an opportunity link will automatically generate after closing the incident.

![](https://files.readme.io/c229968-import_message_or_thread.png)
![](https://files.readme.io/c069c0f-import_message_or_thread.png)
![](https://files.readme.io/6eb4bd7-accept.png)

To gather channel data from additional channel messages or threads, navigate to  **(Slack Message actions)**  **Import message or thread**  **Choose to add to an existing Opportunity**.

Adding information from other channels or threads helps to give more context to where related conversations were happening for the situation.

Step 2: Create a Narrative in the Narrative Builder
---------------------------------------------------

When you open your opportunity in Jeli, navigate to the **Report**  **Narrative** tab.

![](https://files.readme.io/fae5a72-narrative.png)

Click **Edit Narrative** and then **Add Marker** to start telling the story of your incident using the markers:

* **Detection**
* **Diagnosis**
* **Repair**
* **Key Moment**

![](https://files.readme.io/5ac2139-markers.png)

Step 3: Add Narrative Marker Details
------------------------------------

As you skim through the transcript, add additional information to further explain the story.

* Add a **Summary** to your marker to easily explain what is happening.
* Add messages directly from your sources as **Supporting Evidence** to each marker.
* Add **Notes** to each marker to highlight what you want to learn more about (i.e., questions for the review meeting).

![](https://files.readme.io/4b9c956-marker_details.png)

Step 4: Use the People View to Understand who was Involved in the Incident
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

Navigate back to the opportunity and select the **People** tab. The **world map** can help you see how folks coordinated during the incident in different time zones. The **user list** below the map displays who was involved in the incident, and you can use this to ensure your review meeting attendee list is complete.

![](https://files.readme.io/b8f46f3-people.png)

During your Review Meeting
==========================

Step 1: Welcome Attendees by Summarizing the Incident
-----------------------------------------------------

Go through the **Executive Summary** and timing details.

![](https://files.readme.io/7386623-executive_summary.png)

Step 2: Review the Narrative Timeline
-------------------------------------

* Go through your narrative timeline in Jeli with the attendees.
* Bring up the questions you have noted and call on those who participated in the incident.
* Take down any possible action items (you can do this directly in Jeli).
* Clarify any closing thoughts and learnings.

![](https://files.readme.io/8ef7f32-narrative.png)

After Your Meeting
==================

Step 1: Update Takeaways and Action Items
-----------------------------------------

* You may include **Takeaways** and **Action Items** from your meeting directly in their respective tabs in the opportunity.
* You can summarize this information from notes, or delegate to meeting participants who have the best expertise to write them up.

![](https://files.readme.io/ed7478b-takeaways.png)
![](https://files.readme.io/79085ab-action_items.png)

Step 2: Share Your Jeli Investigation with Others in your Organization
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Export your report as a `.pdf`, choosing which parts of it you want to share depending on your audience. Click **Export Report** in the upper right of the opportunity and then use the left hand **toggles** to add/remove **Report Sections**. Click **Export Report** on the left to generate a `.pdf` version.

There are no user limits, so anyone at your company can benefit from your hard work. They’ll be able to see your timelines and notes, as well as explore for themselves.

Check out our [blog post](https://www.jeli.io/blog/techniques-for-sharing-incident-findings) on the different ways you can share incident findings with your organization.

![](https://files.readme.io/bc164c9-Exportreport.gif)
